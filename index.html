Statistical power of Tree-Based Scan Statistics (TBSS) for the surveillance of pregnancy drug safety in Swedish health registers

Tsampá, S.1,  Eriksson, J.1,  Netterström-Wedin, F.1,  Hägg, D.1,  Axfors, C.1,2,3

Abstract

Introduction — With more than two-thirds of pregnant women using prescription drugs, it is a critical concern that drug safety evidence during pregnancy is systematically missing. To bridge this gap, tree-based scan statistics (TBSS) can be used to detect signals across many adverse outcomes in health register data. However, for rare outcomes, TBSS may be underpowered to identify safety alerts, except for those with large effect sizes. Statistical power, which depends on multiple factors, has not been investigated in the context of Nordic register-based data.

Aim — To investigate the statistical power of TBSS to identify alerts related to important infant and maternal adverse events after drug exposure during the first trimester of pregnancy, across various analytical scenarios.

Methods — The outcome tree for this study will include major congenital malformations (according to the EUROCAT classification 1.5), as well as other predefined important infant and maternal adverse outcomes (e.g., preterm birth, preeclampsia). A nationwide cohort drawn from Swedish national register-based data (2006–2022) will be used to calculate outcome prevalences. Statistical power will be defined as the ability to correctly reject a false null hypothesis (of no difference in expected and observed outcome event counts, in simulated data with injected effects). Outcome-specific statistical power for outcomes with varying frequencies will be calculated by combining different model specifications (Poisson- or Bernoulli-based maximum likelihood estimator), outcome tree versions, exposed sample sizes, effect sizes, and alert thresholds. The TBSS R package by Russo and Wang will be used.

Results — Analysis is ongoing. We will present the statistical power of TBSS for the specified factor-level combinations and identify analytical combinations with acceptable power (≥80%).

Conclusion — This study will guide investigators conducting perinatal signal detection studies using TBSS in Nordic registers towards designs with acceptable statistical power for the investigated outcomes, which is crucial to study interpretation and reproducibility.
